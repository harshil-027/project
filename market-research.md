> **The headline number:** 83% of 1.5 million engineering graduates from 2024 left campus without a job or internship. That is approximately **1.25 million students** — in a single year — who needed exactly what this product offers.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>🔍 Why the problem is structural, not cyclical</strong></summary>

<br>

This is not a post-COVID blip or a one-year hiring freeze. The data shows a structural disconnect between what Indian colleges produce and what the market needs:

- **The skills gap is real:** Only 45% of engineering graduates meet industry standards despite 64% being technically "employable" on paper (Wheebox, 2024). The gap between assessment scores and real hiring outcomes reveals that graduates lack soft skills, communication, and the ability to present themselves — exactly what interview coaching addresses.

- **The ATS filter is widening:** Over 65% of global hiring platforms now use AI-based resume screening tools (Online Recruitment Market Report, 2024–2025). Indian companies are adopting the same. A student who doesn't know how to optimise their resume for ATS is invisible before a human even reads their application.

- **The communication barrier is decisive:** 52% of graduates fail interviews not because of technical incompetence but because of poor spoken English or presentation skills. This is precisely what voice interview simulation is built to address.

- **College placements are declining, not recovering:** IIT Bombay's placement rate dropped 13% between 2022–2024. IIT Delhi's dropped 15%. If top-tier institutes are struggling, the situation at Tier-2 and Tier-3 colleges — where the majority of our users come from — is significantly worse.

</details>

## 3. India — The Beachhead Market

<details>
<summary><strong>🎯 Why India first, and why now</strong></summary>

<br>

India is not just the biggest market for this product — it is the most urgent one. Several factors converge to make India the right beachhead:

**1. Volume of the problem**
India produces 1.5 million engineering graduates per year from approximately 5,868 institutions. Even targeting just the English-speaking Tier-2 college segment represents hundreds of thousands of students annually who are actively seeking tools to improve their employability.

**2. Digital nativity**
Indian students in 2025 are highly digitally native. They are active on LinkedIn, GitHub, Reddit (r/developersIndia), and Discord communities. They have already adopted ChatGPT for resume help — but without structure or JD context, the output is inconsistent. Our product provides the guided workflow they're missing.

**3. Price sensitivity creates a moat**
The biggest international competitors — Teal HQ, Resume.io, Jobscan — are priced in USD. At $29–$50/month, they are functionally inaccessible to most Indian students. A ₹299/month price point (~$3.60) is not just competitive; it is a category-creating move that existing players cannot easily replicate without restructuring their entire pricing model.

**4. Community-led growth potential**
Indian students rely heavily on peer recommendations — placement WhatsApp groups, college Discord servers, LinkedIn posts from seniors who got placed. A product that genuinely helps one student land a job will organically reach 20–30 of their peers. This is how we achieve CAC under ₹150.

**5. Hiring trends are shifting**
The IT sector reduced fresher hiring from 600,000 in 2022 to under 150,000 in 2024. However, Global Capability Centres (GCCs) plan to boost fresher hiring by 40%, and AI/ML roles are growing rapidly. Students need to pivot — which means they need tools that can quickly tailor their resume and prep them for the roles that are actually hiring, not the roles that were hiring 3 years ago. Our JD-to-resume alignment feature is built exactly for this.

</details>

## 4. Market Trends & Tailwinds

<details>
<summary><strong>📈 Five tailwinds driving this market right now</strong></summary>

<br>

**1. AI in hiring is now table stakes — not a differentiator**
93% of Indian hiring managers find it acceptable for candidates to use generative AI to refine resumes (IMARC Group, 2024). The stigma around AI-assisted job applications is gone. Students are already using AI — but without the right structure. We provide the structure.

**2. ATS adoption is accelerating in India**
As companies scale their hiring through platforms (Naukri, LinkedIn, Instahyre, Cutshort), ATS filtering becomes the first gate. Students who don't understand ATS are invisible. Demand for ATS optimisation tools is rising in direct proportion to job board adoption.

**3. The GCC boom creates new role demand**
Global Capability Centres are expanding rapidly in India, targeting a 40% increase in fresher hiring. These roles — product analysts, SWE, data engineers — require strong written communication, structured interview performance, and role-specific preparation. Generic resume tools don't serve this.

**4. Communication skills are the decisive differentiator**
52% of graduates fail interviews not on technical skills but on communication. This is a validated pain point that creates direct demand for voice interview simulation. The market is not saturated with tools that address this — particularly at a price point accessible to students.

**5. Community-driven job searching is the norm**
Indian students increasingly get job leads from community networks, not just job boards. This means virality is built into the distribution — a student who gets a job using our tool will naturally share it in the WhatsApp group where they got the job lead.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>⚠️ Headwinds to be aware of</strong></summary>

<br>

| Headwind | Impact | Our response |

## 6. Go-To-Market Implications

<details>
<summary><strong>🚀 What this research tells us about GTM strategy</strong></summary>

<br>

The market research points to five clear GTM conclusions:

**1. Community-led, not paid-ad-led**
The primary acquisition channel should be Indian student communities — r/developersIndia, placement WhatsApp groups, LinkedIn posts from recent graduates, college tech clubs. CAC through community is near-zero; through paid ads in India it would be ₹200–400+ per install. We can't afford that with a ₹299 product.

**2. SEO is a long-term asset**
Searches like "how to tailor resume to job description India", "IIT placement preparation 2025", "SWE interview prep India free" have high intent and relatively low competition. A content strategy targeting these terms builds organic traffic that compounds.

**3. The .edu free trial is the student acquisition hook**
Offering 3 months free with a valid college email address is a direct distribution mechanism into college networks. One student gets it free → tells their placement group → 20 sign up. The lifetime value of that community far exceeds the cost of 3 months Pro.

**4. Time the launch to placement season**
Campus placements in India peak between August–December and January–March. Launching or intensifying marketing in July–August targets students at their highest anxiety and highest motivation to try tools. A March launch means we have one cycle to learn before the peak season.

**5. B2B in Year 2, not Year 1**
College placement cells and career advisors are the B2B channel — but only after the product is proven B2C. Approaching colleges with an unproven tool gets a polite no. Approaching them with "we've helped 5,000 students from your region improve their resumes" gets a meeting.

</details>
