## 1. The Problem

<details>
<summary><strong>😟 What students face every job cycle</strong></summary>

<br>

Every year, millions of students and fresh graduates enter one of the most competitive job markets in history. They face a brutal combination of challenges that compound on each other:

- They spend hours on a resume they're genuinely unsure is good enough
- They apply to 30–50 jobs with the **same generic resume** — not tailored to any single JD
- They have **no credible way to practice interviews** before the real thing
- The tools that could help them cost **$20–$40/month** — a real barrier for a student

The result: talented candidates are filtered out before a human ever reads their work. Not because they're unqualified — but because their resume didn't match what the ATS or recruiter was scanning for, and they froze in the first interview round from lack of practice.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>🔍 Why existing tools fail them</strong></summary>

<br>

| Tool | What it does | Where it fails |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Resume.io / Canva | Resume templates & formatting | No AI feedback, no JD alignment |
| Grammarly | Spelling & grammar | Cannot evaluate impact, metrics, or role-fit |
| Teal / Jobscan | ATS keyword scanning | Expensive, no interview coaching |
| Interview Warmup (Google) | Pre-scripted interview questions | Not role-specific, no dynamic follow-ups |
| ChatGPT (DIY) | Can do some of the above | Requires expert prompting; no guided flow |

**The gap:** No single affordable tool closes the loop from *"I have a resume"* to *"I am ready for this interview."*

</details>

## 2. The Opportunity

<details>
<summary><strong>📈 Market context</strong></summary>

<br>

- There are **~300 million** students enrolled in higher education globally, with a large and growing English-speaking segment in India, Southeast Asia, and Africa
- India alone produces **~1.5 million engineering graduates** per year — the majority of whom struggle to differentiate themselves in the job market
- The global online recruitment market is projected to exceed **$43 billion by 2027**
- AI career tools are a rapidly growing category, but **almost none are priced for students**

**The beachhead:** English-speaking students in India (IITs, NITs, and Tier-2 colleges) who are targeting product, engineering, and business roles at tech companies. This is a massive, digitally-native, highly motivated segment that is currently underserved by price-prohibitive Western tools.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>💡 Why now</strong></summary>

<br>

- LLM quality has crossed a threshold where AI-generated resume feedback and interview simulation are genuinely useful — not just novelty
- Students are already using ChatGPT for resume help — but without structure, JD context, or a guided flow, the output is inconsistent
- The cost of building and running AI-powered feedback has dropped dramatically, making a ₹299/month price point viable with healthy margins
- Post-2023 tech layoffs have pushed even experienced candidates back to basics — validating the demand for resume + interview tools across experience levels

</details>

## 3. The Solution

<details>
<summary><strong>🛠️ What we're building</strong></summary>

<br>

**AI Resume + Interview Coach** is a web-based, freemium AI tool with two tightly integrated pillars:

### Pillar 1 — Resume Intelligence
The user uploads their resume and pastes a target job description. The AI:
- Scores the resume against the JD (0–100 match score)
- Rewrites weak bullet points using the XYZ framework
- Reviews every section (Summary, Skills, Education, Experience) with actionable feedback
- Flags ATS compatibility issues

### Pillar 2 — Interview Coaching
The user selects a role type and round. The AI:
- Runs a dynamic mock interview (not a scripted quiz)
- Follows up on answers the way a real interviewer would
- Scores responses on Relevance, Structure, Depth, and Clarity
- Delivers a detailed debrief at the end
- Offers a voice mode for realistic audio practice (Phase 2)

**The end-to-end flow:**
```
Upload Resume → Paste JD → See Match Score → Fix Resume → Practice Interview → Walk in Confident
```

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>🎯 The "aha moment"</strong></summary>

<br>

The product is designed to deliver value within the **first 5 minutes** of use:

1. User uploads resume *(30 seconds)*
2. User pastes a job description they're excited about *(1 minute)*
3. AI returns a match score — say, 41/100 — with a breakdown *(8 seconds)*
4. AI shows exactly which bullet points are weak and rewrites two of them *(free)*
5. User sees their score jump to 67/100 after accepting the suggestions

That moment — seeing your resume visibly improve in under 5 minutes — is the hook. Everything else (interview coaching, voice mode, version history) is the depth that converts free users to paid.

</details>

## 4. Differentiators

<details>
<summary><strong>⚡ What makes this different from every other tool</strong></summary>

<br>

| Differentiator | What it means in practice |
|---------------|--------------------------|
| **Hyper-personalised to the JD** | Every piece of feedback — resume and interview — is generated with the specific job description as context. Not generic advice. |
| **Affordable by design** | ₹299/month (~$3.60). Built for students with no budget. Pro is priced at volume, not margin. |
| **End-to-end in one place** | Resume improvement → interview practice → debrief, all without switching tools or tabs |
| **Dynamic interviews, not scripts** | The AI follows up on your actual answer. It can ask "Can you give me a specific example?" just like a real interviewer. |
| **Role-specific depth** | SWE, PM, Design, Finance, Marketing question banks — not one-size-fits-all HR questions |

</details>

## 5. Target Market

<details>
<summary><strong>🎯 Primary market — Indian students & fresh graduates</strong></summary>

<br>

- **Who:** Final-year students and graduates (0–2 years experience) from engineering, business, and design backgrounds
- **Where:** Primarily India — Tier-1 and Tier-2 colleges; secondarily Southeast Asia and international students in the US/UK
- **Income:** Low disposable income; highly price-sensitive; willing to pay if they see proof of value
- **Behaviour:** Heavy LinkedIn and GitHub users; familiar with AI tools; active on Reddit/Discord job-seeking communities
- **Pain intensity:** High — getting a job is a life-changing event; they are highly motivated to try tools that help

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>🌍 Secondary market — Career switchers & recent graduates globally</strong></summary>

<br>

- **Who:** Professionals with 1–4 years of experience pivoting to a new domain (e.g., marketing → PM, finance → tech)
- **Where:** Global English-speaking markets
- **Key need:** Reframing existing experience through the lens of the target role — a use case our JD alignment feature directly addresses
- **Willingness to pay:** Higher than students; $4.99/month is very low friction for this segment

</details>

## 6. Business Model Summary

<details>
<summary><strong>💳 Freemium — free is real, paid is deep</strong></summary>

<br>

The freemium model is the strategic backbone. The principle: **a student must be able to get real, meaningful value without paying.** The paid tier offers depth and volume, not access to the core loop.

**Free tier:** Resume upload, 3 JD scans/month, 5 bullet rewrites/session, 2 mock interviews/month (1 domain)

**Pro tier — ₹299/month (~$4.99):** Unlimited everything + voice interviews + all 5 domains + version history + full ATS report

**Revenue model:** Freemium SaaS with potential B2B licensing to colleges and bootcamps in Phase 3

**Unit economics target:**
- CAC: < ₹150 (organic/referral-led growth)
- LTV: ₹900 (avg 3-month subscription per job seeker)
- LTV:CAC ratio target: > 6x

</details>
